The Synergetics operating system proposes a novel paradigm for operating system (OS) design inspired by the principles of Synergetics and ephemeralization, as developed by Buckminster Fuller. Traditional operating systems rely on hierarchical, reductionist architectures grounded in deterministic logic and linear resource management.

Design Philosophy

The SOS Paradigm

In contrast, a Synergetic Operating System (SOS) reimagines computation as a holistic, relational, and geometry-driven system, enabling adaptive behavior, resource efficiency, and emergent optimization.

The objective is not to replace mathematical computation, but to elevate system design philosophy, allowing operating systems to evolve toward self-organizing, energy-aware, and topology-native computational environments.
